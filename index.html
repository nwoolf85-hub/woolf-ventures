V.   The Story
The House

Two brothers.
One long game.

Woolf Ventures isn't a startup. It's a house — a body of work that two brothers from Ann Arbor, Michigan decided to build under one roof instead of scattering across a dozen jobs for a dozen other people's companies.

The company was formed in Austin in 2025, but the story started a long time before that, in a different town. Neal and Steven grew up in Ann Arbor — a place that teaches you that the quiet, unfashionable pursuits are the ones that age well. Neal followed the craft beer industry down to Austin in 2019, stayed for the people and the pace, and spent the next several years running B2B sales in the Central Texas convenience-store market — meeting store owners face-to-face, learning which products move at 11pm versus 11am, and watching the industry try and fail to digitize itself from the outside. Steven went deep on AI and systems, advising operators and attorneys on how to turn ambiguous human intent into verified outcomes. Different lanes. Same instinct: build the tool you always wished you had, then hand it to people who need it.

At some point it stopped making sense to keep running the plays for other people. So we put the name on the door, wrote the thesis down, and started stacking ventures under it. Built in Austin. Raised in Ann Arbor. Running together.

The Thesis

Every venture in this house sits at the intersection of four things: sales, technology, story, and sound. Sales is the street knowledge — the part you only learn by losing deals. Technology is the leverage — the part that lets a two-person house operate like a twenty-person one. Story is the frame — the part that makes people care before they understand. Sound is the soul — the part that gives the work a pulse you remember after you close the tab.

Most companies lead with one corner and bolt the others on when they need them. We lead with all four at once because the unusual combination is the whole moat. Anybody with a pulse can copy a feature. Nobody can copy the way two brothers with complementary obsessions pay attention to the same problem.

“We didn't come here to build a company.
We came here to build a body of work.”
— The House Rule
What's Under The Roof

Tasty Trends is the B2B sales platform serving convenience stores across Central Texas. Drip Austin is a consumer brand of premium portion-cup dipping sauces launching in 2026. Woolf Consulting is how we rent out the playbook by the evening. Conquest of Empire, Music & Voice, and Patents & IP are the longer bets — original screenplays, scored work, and filed ideas that will surface in their own time. The Portfolio page has the whole list, but the list is less interesting than the fact that it all lives in one building and shares one memory.

Why It Matters

Because houses outlast jobs. Because the operators who do their best work are the ones who own the whole playbook — not just the part their title says they're allowed to touch. Because in a market where every tool is converging on the same middle, unusual combinations are the only real edge left. And because, honestly, we've been running together our whole lives. This is just the version where we get to put our name on the door.
