Thomas Woolf

Pharmaceutical patent attorney. Forty years on record. Owns a landline.

Thomas Woolf has been practicing pharmaceutical patent law in New York City since before Steve and Neal were old enough to know what a patent was. He lives in Little Falls, New Jersey, and commutes into Manhattan to work with the kind of high-powered M&A counsel whose names you would recognize and whose hourly rates you would not believe. He has seen patent strategies come and go. He has seen clients come and go. He has seen a great many brilliant ideas that were, on closer inspection, neither brilliant nor particularly good ideas.

He was the first person in his family to go to college. He graduated at the top of his class at Washington University, then did it again at Johns Hopkins, where he earned his Ph.D. Somewhere along the way he wrote the Handbook of Drug Metabolism , which is still in print and still being cited by people who would rather reinvent it from scratch. He then spent several decades turning scientific insight into defensible intellectual property — which is a polite way of saying he spent several decades telling clients that their favorite claim was unenforceable and they should draft a better one.

Today, Steve and Neal run Woolf Ventures. Tom is Of Counsel, in the formal sense of the term — senior, associated, available, and not running anything. He doesn't need to. The brothers grew up watching how he thinks, which turns out to be most of the inheritance. The name on the door does the rest.

Things Tom approves of

A well-drafted term sheet. A conservatively modeled forecast. Reading the actual documents before the call. A lit cigar. A quiet room. A landline.

Things Tom does not approve of

Most things.
